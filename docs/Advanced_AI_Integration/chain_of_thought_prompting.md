## Chain-Of-Thought (CoT) Prompting: 

> User:
> 
> Consider this scenario:

> In a school, there are three children having lunch. Each child has one apple, two sandwiches, and three bottles of water.
> If each child takes a unique item and trades it with another child for their unique item, what is one possibility of items each child could end up having?

> Assistant: Let's label the kids **A**, **B**, and **C**.
>
> Each child starts with:
> * 1 apple
> * 2 sandwiches
> * 3 bottles of water
>
> Assume we call them Child A, Child B, and Child C.
>
> Now suppose each chooses one _different_ type of item to trade:
> * Child A trades their **apple**
> * Child B trades one **sandwich**
> * Child C trades one **bottle of water**
>
> They each trade with someone who chose a different item.
> 
> **One possible trade arrangement:**
> * Child A gives their apple to Child B
